Plans to Save Honey Production

Ever since 2006, the production of honey has declined. This is because of the rapid increase of bees leaving or dying off from their colonies. This has also caused concern for pollinating food crops. Since around 20 to 40 percent of bees in each colony are disappearing from their colonies, this puts the $15 million food crops in jeopardy. This has caused the White House to call into action a strategy to help save the nations bees from this colony collapse disorder. The strategy is called the National Strategy to Promote the Health of Honey Bees and Other Pollinators
